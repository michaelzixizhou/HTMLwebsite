About Me

Hi! I'm a Toronto-based programmer currently attending University of Toronto for Neuroscience and Computer Science.

I am incredibly passionate about computer science and its potential to make a positive impact on people's lives. My journey in this field began in 2022. Since then, I have fallen in love with the limitless possibilities of programming.

What truly drives me is the desire to merge my interests in neuroscience and CS. I envision using neurotech to create opportunities for medical and entertainment purposes.

  • January 2022
    First contact
    My initial encounter with programming, specifically through my first language, Python, at the University of Toronto, felt remarkably natural. As I delved into the world of coding, I experienced an immediate sense of alignment with the logic and structure inherent in programming.
  • July 2022
    Website!
    Embarking on the journey of creating my first website was a thrilling experience that resonated with my creative side. As I crafted each line of code, the website gradually took shape, capturing my vision and allowing me to showcase my ideas to the world.
  • November 2022
    The competition begins
    Participating in both the Newhacks and Cybersci competitions over a single weekend was an exhilarating experience that pushed me to the limits of my abilities. It was an honour competing alongside UofTCTF, and we ended up placing third and fifth place at Cybersci 2022!
  • January 2023
    New beginnings
    Transitioning from neuroscience to computer science and securing a place in the highly competitive CS program at the University of Toronto was a defining moment in my academic journey. I dedicated countless hours to self-study, mastering programming languages, and expanding my technical expertise. UofTHacks X was a nice little break in between.
  • May 2023
    COA Education Java tutor
    Embarking on the role of a Java tutor for a student became a transformative experience where I not only helped them grasp the intricacies of programming but also had the opportunity to reshape their curriculum. Teaching is a joy that never ceases to inspire and invigorate me. Witnessing the moment when a student's face lights up with understanding, when they grasp a concept or solve a challenging problem, is an immensely rewarding experience.

Portfolio

A MS Paint alternative for MacOS. Built with Python using pygame, this tool allows for drawing, writing text, and pasting images to doodle on!

Made for UofTHacks X, In A Flash is a flashcard service prototype that explores the concept of connecting curriculums worldwide as one.

Made for NewHacks2022, TrackTC uses TTC alert API to inform users of delays for their chosen trains and buses. TrackTC uses a Django-React stack with the DjangoRESTFramework.

Built with Unity2D, Tile Game involves grid-based movement in a classic RPG style. The range of each unit is displayed in a flood fill algorithm. Work in progress, art by yours truly.

Infinite Scroller is a mobile game that utilizes Unity2D and ShaderLab scripting to generate seamless gameplay.

MusicMetrics is the fruit of an 8-person collaboration. Built completely using Java, MusicMetrics can be adapted to store music data from any API, and present it to users. SOLID and Clean Architecture were used to organize the project and create maintainable code.
